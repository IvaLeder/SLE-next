### Steps

<Steps title="How to crush a can with air pressure">
<Step title="Add the water">Pour **one tablespoon** of water into an empty soda can.</Step>
<Step title="Heat it up">Place the can on the stove until the water boils and you see steam rising from the opening.</Step>
<Step>Using tongs, quickly flip the can upside-down into a bowl of ice water and watch it crush instantly.</Step>
</Steps>

### Term

The steam inside the can <Term def="Water vapor turning back into liquid water.">condenses</Term> the moment it touches cold water.

### Key takeaways

<KeyTakeaways>
- Air pressure is strong enough to crush a can.
- Steam condensing creates a sudden pressure drop.
- Always let an adult handle the hot can.
</KeyTakeaways>

### FAQ

<FAQ>
<Question question="How long does this experiment take?">
About **10 minutes**, including setup and cleanup.
</Question>
<Question question="Can I do it without a stove?">
Yes — a camping burner works too, but it must produce real steam, so a hair dryer won't do.
</Question>
</FAQ>
